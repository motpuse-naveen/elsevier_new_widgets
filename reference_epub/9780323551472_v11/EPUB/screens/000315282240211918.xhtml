G Essay Question Group

State whether the following sentences are true or false, and explain your answers.

State whether the following sentences are true or false, and explain your answers.

Question 1

1.

After a total (complete) hysterectomy, a woman still has regular menstrual periods.

Feedback

False. Total hysterectomy means removal of the entire uterus so that menstruation does not occur.

Question 2

2.

After a total hysterectomy, a woman may still produce estrogen and progesterone.

Feedback

True. Total hysterectomy does not mean that the ovaries have been removed.

Question 3

3.

Birth control pills prevent pregnancy by keeping levels of estrogen and progesterone high.

Feedback

True. Birth control pills contain estrogen and progesterone; high levels prevent ovulation and pregnancy.

Question 4

4.

After a total hysterectomy with bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy, a doctor may advise hormone replacement therapy.

Feedback

True. This may be necessary to treat symptoms of estrogen loss (vaginal atrophy, hot flashes) and to prevent bone deterioration (osteoporosis).

Question 5

5.

Human papillomavirus can cause genital warts and ovarian cancer.

Feedback

False. HPV does produce genital warts but not ovarian cancer. In some cases, HPV infection may lead to cervical cancer.

Question 6

6.

A Pap test can detect cervical dysplasia.

Feedback

True. A Pap test can detect abnormal changes in the cervix from cervical dysplasia to cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN) and CIS (carcinoma in situ).

Question 7

7.

Human chorionic gonadotropin is produced by the ovaries during pregnancy.

Feedback

False. The hormone hCG is produced by the placenta during pregnancy.

Question 8

8.

Gynecomastia is a common condition in pregnant women.

Feedback

False. Gynecomastia is a condition of increased breast development in males .

Question 9

9.

Treatment for endometriosis is uterine myomectomy.

Feedback

False. Myomectomy means removal of muscle tumors (fibroids). Endometriosis is abnormal location of uterine tissue outside the uterine lining.

Question 10

10.

A gravida 3 para 2 is a woman who has given birth 3 times.

Feedback

False. A gravida 3 para 2 is a woman who has had two children and three pregnancies.

Question 11

11.

A nulligravida is a woman who has had several pregnancies.

Feedback

False. A nulligravida has had no pregnancies. A multigravida has had many pregnancies.

Question 12

12.

Pseudocyesis is the same condition as a tubal pregnancy.

Feedback

False. A pseudocyesis is a false pregnancy (no pregnancy occurs), and a tubal pregnancy is an example of ectopic pregnancy (pregnancy occurs in the fallopian tube, not in the uterus).

Question 13

13.

Fibrocystic changes in the breast are a malignant condition.

Feedback

False. Fibrocystic changes in the breast are a benign condition.

Question 14

14.

Cystadenomas occur in the ovaries.

Feedback

True. Cystadenomas are glandular sacs lined with tumor cells; they occur in the ovaries.

Question 15

15.

FSH and LH are ovarian hormones.

Feedback

False. FSH and LH are pituitary gland hormones. Estrogen and progesterone are secreted by the ovaries.

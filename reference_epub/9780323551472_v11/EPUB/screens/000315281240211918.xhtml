G Essay Question Group

Give short answers for the following.

Give short answers for the following.

Question 1

1.

What is the difference between hematuria and uremia?

Feedback

Hematuria is the presence of blood in the urine, and uremia is a toxic condition of excess urea (nitrogenous waste) in the bloodstream. Hematuria is a symptomatic condition of the urine (-uria), and uremia is an abnormal condition of the blood (-emia).

Question 2

2.

What is diuresis?

Feedback

Diuresis is the excessive production of urine (polyuria).

Question 3

3.

What is a diuretic?

Feedback

A diuretic is a drug or chemical (caffeine or alcohol) that causes diuresis to occur.

Question 4

4.

What is antidiuretic hormone?

Feedback

Antidiuretic hormone is a hormone produced by the pituitary gland that normally helps the renal tubules to reabsorb water back into the bloodstream. It works against diuresis to help retain water in the blood.

Question 5

5.

What is hyponatremia?

Feedback

Hyponatremia is abnormally low levels of sodium in the bloodstream.

Question 6

6.

What is hyperkalemia?

Feedback

Hyperkalemia is abnormally high concentration of potassium in the blood. The major cause is chronic renal failure.

Question 7

7.

What is PKU?

Feedback

PKU is phenylketonuria. This occurs when there are high levels of phenylketones in urine and phenylalanine in the blood. The condition causes mental retardation in infants.

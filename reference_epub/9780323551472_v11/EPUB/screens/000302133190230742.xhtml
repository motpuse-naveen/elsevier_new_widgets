CASE REPORT

Fill-in-Blank Dropdown Question Group

Four-year-old Sally has been running a low-grade fever for several weeks, with recurrent sore throat, earache, and cough. Her mother takes her to the family physician, who diagnoses her condition as otitis. Sally continues to be fatigued and anorexic. Her mother then notices bruising on her legs and arms. The family physician fi nally orders blood tests and an antibiotic drug. Peripheral blood tests reveal Hgb 7.4, platelet count 40,000, and WBC count 85,000 with 90% lymphoblasts. A bone marrow biopsy is ordered.

Question 1

1. What's the likely diagnosis for this patient?   CLL / CML / AML / ALL  

2. The probable cause of Sally’s ecchymoses is:   Otitis / Neutropenia / Thrombocytopenia / Anorexia  

3. The likely explanation for Sally’s fatigue is:   Neutropenia / Sore throat and cough / Anemia  

4. Treatment for Sally’s condition is likely to be:   Intravenous feeding / Surgery to repair the bone marrow / Prolonged antibiotic therapy / Chemotherapy

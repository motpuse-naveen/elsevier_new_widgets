Question 1

1.

Microscopic projections in the walls of the small intestine:

Feedback

 Papillae are nipple-like projections in the tongue where taste buds are located, and rugae are folds in the mucous membrane of the stomach and hard palate. 

Question 2

2.

salivary gland near the ear:

Feedback

The submandibular gland is under the lower jaw, and the sublingual gland is under the tongue. 

Question 3

3.

ring of muscle at the end of the stomach:

Feedback

The uvula is soft tissue hanging from the soft palate, and the lower esophageal sphincter is a ring of muscle between the esophagus and stomach. 

Question 4

4.

soft, inner section of a tooth:

Feedback

Dentin is the hard part of the tooth directly under the enamel and in the root, and enamel is the hard, outermost part of the tooth composing the crown.

Question 5

5.

Chemical that speeds up reactions and helps digest foods:

Feedback

A triglyceride is a large fat molecule, and an amino acid is a substance produced when proteins are digested.

Question 6

6.

pigment released with bile:

Feedback

Glycogen is animal starch that is produced in liver cells from sugar, and melena is dark, tarry stools.

Question 7

7.

hormone produced by endocrine cells of the pancreas:

Feedback

Amylase and lipase are digestive enzymes produced by the exocrine cells of the pancreas. 

Question 8

8.

 rhythm-like contraction of the muscles in the walls of the gastrointestinal tract:

Feedback

Deglutition is swallowing, and mastication is chewing.

Question 9

9.

breakdown of large fat globules:

Feedback

 Absorption is the passage of materials through the walls of the small intestine into the bloodstream, and anabolism is the process of building up proteins in a cell (protein synthesis). 

Question 10

10.

 pointed, dog-like tooth medial to premolars:

Feedback

 An incisor is one of the four front teeth in the dental arch (not pointed or like a dog’s tooth), and a molar is any of three large teeth just behind (distal to) the two premolar teeth.

What is jaundice?

Feedback

yellow-orange coloration of the skin and other tissues (hyperbilirubinemia)

Question 2

2.

List three ways in which a patient can become jaundiced:

Feedback

a. any liver disease (hepatopathy—such as cirrhosis, hepatoma, or hepatitis), so that bilirubin is not processed into bile and cannot be excreted in feces

b. obstruction of bile flow, so that bile and bilirubin are not excreted and accumulate in the bloodstream

c. excessive hemolysis leading to overproduction of bilirubin and high levels in the bloodstream
